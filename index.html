The Program

A structured path from curiosity to proof

Three phases. One mentor. A real project that becomes the strongest part of your university application.

What you build

Students choose a real problem in their field of interest and work with their PhD mentor to produce a tangible, evidence-based output:

  • A research paper or literature review
  • A working prototype or software tool
  • A designed solution with tested evidence

What you leave with

  • Polished academic report
  • Evidence-based CV bullets
  • Personal statement material
  • Interview answers bank with mock prep
  • 2-minute pitch + Q&A readiness
Phase I

SPARK - Find a strong project

Learn core skills, find direction, and commit to a real problem.

  • Kickoff & orientation with your PhD mentor
  • Core theory sessions & guided discussions
  • Problem framing - define context, constraints, success indicators
  • Research basics & critical thinking foundations
  • Writing lab - clear structure, logical flow
  • Project plan - goals, methodology, tools, expected outcome
Phase II

BUILD - Create, test, and refine

Build the main output, validate it, and document clearly.

  • Build sprint - prototype, implement, or draft research output
  • Evidence sprint - run validation, capture structured evidence
  • Writing lab - interpret results, integrate sources
  • Iteration & documentation - refine based on evidence
Phase III

SHOWCASE - Present and defend

Turn the project into a strong application story.

  • Academic report polish - strengthen logic, clean citations
  • Pitch + public speaking + live Q&A prep
  • Application pack build - CV, personal statement, interview bank
  • Mock interviews modelled on top universities' formats

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything students and parents need to know about the Masarak program.

What exactly does the Masarak program include?

The program pairs you with a PhD mentor from a top university for a structured, three-phase journey: Spark (find and frame a project), Build (create and test a real output), and Showcase (turn it into a polished application story). You leave with a completed project, an academic report, CV material, personal statement content, and an interview answers bank.

Who are the mentors?

All Masarak mentors are PhD researchers or graduates from world-leading universities including Cambridge, Oxford, Edinburgh, and Imperial. Each mentor is matched to a student based on their field of interest - from engineering and computer science to biomedical sciences and beyond.

When should my child start?

Ideally 12-18 months before your university application deadline. This allows time to complete a meaningful project, develop strong application material, and prepare for interviews. Most families join in Year 11 or the equivalent, but earlier starts give more flexibility.

How long is the program?

The core program runs over several months across three phases. The exact timeline depends on the student's goals and chosen project, but most students complete the full Spark-Build-Showcase cycle within one academic term. Sessions are typically weekly with your mentor.

Is the program online or in person?

Mentorship sessions run online, which means students can join from anywhere. We also plan in-person programs and events in Riyadh - register your interest to be the first to know about the next available cohort.

How are parents kept in the loop?

We schedule regular touch points with parents throughout the program. You will receive progress updates, have opportunities to ask questions, and always know where your child stands. Communication and transparency are central to how we work.

What kind of project will my child work on?

Projects are tailored to each student's interests and target field. Examples include building a software tool, conducting a research investigation, designing an engineering prototype, or writing a structured literature review. The output is real and tangible - not a simulation.

What grades or qualifications does my child need to join?

There are no strict grade requirements. We look for motivated students who are serious about applying to competitive universities. Whether your child is in the Saudi national curriculum, international school, or studying A-Levels or IB, we tailor the program to fit.

Do you help with UK university applications specifically?

Yes. Our core focus is helping students build standout applications for top UK universities - including UCAS personal statements, Oxbridge and medicine interviews, and admissions tests. The project, CV, and interview prep are all designed with UK admissions in mind.

My child doesn't know what they want to study yet - can they still join?

Yes - that is one of the best times to start. The Spark phase is specifically designed to help students explore their interests and discover a direction. Students who begin undecided often finish with a clear subject choice backed by a real project.

How much does the program cost?

Pricing depends on the package and duration. Book a free consultation call and we will walk you through the options and help you choose the right fit for your child's goals and timeline.

Does my child need prior research or project experience?

Not at all. The program is designed to take students from zero experience to a completed, university-quality project. Your mentor guides every step - from choosing a topic to writing the final report.
